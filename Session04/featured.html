Polyozellus atrolazulinus — Blue Chanterelle

Genus: Polyozellus

Species: atrolazulinus

Type: False Gill

This specimen yields deep, muted tones when used as a dye. The surface texture and layered gills create visual interest.

Polyozellus atrolazulinus is a rare and striking species of mushroom often called the "blue chanterelle" because of its deep blue to violet-black coloring. Unlike many mushrooms that grow singly, it forms large, clustered rosettes. It is usually found in coniferous forests in mossy or shaded areas, mainly in East Asia and North America. While its edibility is unclear, it is prized for its unique pigments and potential medicinal compounds.
