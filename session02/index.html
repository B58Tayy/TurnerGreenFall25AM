Most coral mushrooms (Artomyces pyxidatus) are edible mushrooms. They’re also known as crown-tipped coral mushrooms, crown coral, or crown-tipped coral fungus. As their name suggests, these mushrooms look very similar to coral found in reefs or the ocean floor.

Coral mushrooms are less well-researched than other mushrooms. However, they likely contain similar nutrient profiles such as fiber, protein, trace nutrients, and minerals. Some studies suggest that coral mushrooms may have antimicrobial and antiviral properties, though more research is needed. Recent scientific interest also explores their potential role in supporting cognitive health, including reducing proteins linked with Alzheimer’s disease.
